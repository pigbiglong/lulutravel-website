3. About Visas

Most travelers require a visa to enter mainland China. We recommend applying for a L (Tourist) Visa through your nearest Chinese embassy or consulate.

Currently, citizens of select countries enjoy visa-free entry for stays up to 15 days. Please check the latest regulations or contact us for current visa requirements specific to your nationality.

We provide invitation letters and all necessary documentation to support your visa application.

The visa application process typically involves:

  1. Complete the online visa application form (COVA)
  2. Prepare required documents: passport, photo, flight itinerary, hotel bookings
  3. Submit application at Chinese Visa Application Service Center
  4. Pay visa fee and wait for processing (typically 4-7 working days)

We provide detailed visa guidance after booking, including invitation letters and support documents. For complex cases, we can recommend visa agencies.

Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned departure date from China and have at least two blank visa pages.

If your passport is nearing expiration, we recommend renewing it before applying for your Chinese visa, as visas cannot be transferred between passports.

No hidden fees. We believe in transparent pricing. The tour price you see is what you pay for all included services.

Any optional activities or upgrades are clearly marked as such. We never pressure travelers into purchases or take commission-based shopping stops.

Yes, comprehensive travel insurance is mandatory for all our tours. This protects both you and the group in case of unexpected circumstances.

Your insurance must cover:

  • Emergency medical expenses (minimum $100,000)

China has excellent medical facilities in major cities, often with Western-trained doctors.
